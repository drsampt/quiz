Inside Physical Therapy — The Clinical Progression
How treatment actually moves from diagnosis to full training confidence
Point A
Frustrated & Limited
Lifts feel off, painful, or unstable. Progress is stalled. Confidence under heavy weight is slipping.
1
Assessment & Game Plan
Identify where lifts break down, what positions are lost, and why issues appear under load. Analyze competition lifts and compensation patterns.
2
Fix Weak Links & Reinforce Positions
Target muscles and movement patterns that fail during heavy lifts. Rebuild control and confidence in weak positions without shutting training down.
3
Reload the Barbell Strategically
Rebuild confidence under the bar using tempo, positional variations, and intelligent loading. Reinforce bracing and positioning before pushing weight.
4
Build Strength That Carries Over
Progress strength and hypertrophy through ranges and movements that directly support squat, bench, and deadlift. Integrate gym-based lifts seamlessly.
5
Bulletproof & Maintain
Establish recovery, accessory selection, and movement strategies that reduce setbacks over time. Build long-term training resilience and consistency.
Point B
Confident & Consistent
Squat, bench, and deadlift feel solid. Accessories have purpose. Recovery is intentional. Training decisions are proactive, not reactive.
